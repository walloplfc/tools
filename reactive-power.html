Technical Reference

Active Power (P)

Real power consumed and converted to useful work (heat, light, mechanical energy). Measured in Megawatts (MW).

P = V × I × cos(φ)

Reactive Power (Q)

Power stored and released by inductors/capacitors. Essential to sustain electromagnetic fields. Measured in Megavolt-Ampere Reactive (MVAR).

Q = P × tan(φ)

Power Factor (PF)

Ratio of active to apparent power. Ranges 0 to 1. PF = 1.0 means purely resistive (no reactive power). Measured in Megavolt-Ampere (MVA) for apparent power.

PF = P / S = cos(φ)

Input Guidelines: Power Factor must be between 0 and 1 (inclusive). A PF of 1.0 yields Q = 0 MVAR (purely resistive load). Lagging PF (inductive: motors, transformers) — Q is positive, system absorbs reactive power. Leading PF (capacitive: capacitor banks, long cables) — Q is negative, system generates reactive power. Active Power input unit is MW; result Q in MVAR; apparent power S in MVA.
